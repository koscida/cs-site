Creative Brief


I have always been extremely interested in interface design and usability. In CS, Iâ€™m studying Human-Centered-Computing and I have spent a lot of time researching what goes into good design. It seems that there is an intuitive element that goes into good design that makes it feel like someone has to be born with. In my thesis project, I want to analyze different websites and really study what goes into making them. I want to see of there is a formula for good design.

I heard once that Google spend hundreds of thousands of dollars researching the exact color of blue that their links should be when they gave search results, to optimize how often users would actually click on them. Facebook blue is almost instantly recognizable and besides black and white it the only color the site uses. Blue, it seams, is the color of the Internet and the choices websites as large as Facebook and Google would not make these choices lightly.

Site layout is something that is designers spend tons of time trying to perfect, so I wonder if there is a sort of golden ratio to create the best website. Is there a common padding and margin size that makes text more readable? Is horizontal navigation always the best? Do sites have the same functionality for active, hovering, or visited links? Is there a standard, or does it depend on the website?

This project will be web-based and will be displayed with the other web-based thesis projects. For the project, I plan to display each of these websites as a common user sees them and once they click around of hover over parts, the div size, padding, margin will be displayed, showing the behind-the-scenes of what goes into the website. At the end of the demos, I would like to create the average or common denominator website that employs the commonalities between all the sites I displayed before.
