Creative Brief

I have always been extremely interested in interface design and usability. In CS, I'm studying Human-Centered-Computing and I have spent a lot of time researching what goes into good design. It seems that there is an intuitive element that goes into good design that makes it feel like someone has to be born with. In my thesis project, I want to analyze different websites and really study what goes into making them. I want to see of there is a formula for good design.

I heard once that Google spend hundreds of thousands of dollars researching the exact color of blue that their links should be when they gave search results, to optimize how often users would actually click on them. Facebook blue is almost instantly recognizable and besides black and white it the only color the site uses. Blue, it seams, is the color of the Internet and the choices websites as large as Facebook and Google would not make these choices lightly.

Site layout is something that is designers spend tons of time trying to perfect, so I wonder if there is a sort of golden ratio to create the best website. Is there a common padding and margin size that makes text more readable? Is horizontal navigation always the best? Do sites have the same functionality for active, hovering, or visited links? Is there a standard, or does it depend on the website?

This project will be web-based and will be displayed with the other web-based thesis projects. For the project, I plan to display each of these websites as a common user sees them and once they click around of hover over parts, the div size, padding, margin will be displayed, showing the behind-the-scenes of what goes into the website. At the end of the demos, I would like to create the average or common denominator website that employs the commonalities between all the sites I displayed before.

Project Description

Popular Styling

Brittany Kos

According to Alexa.com, the top visited websites in America are Google, Facebook, YouTube, Yahoo, Wikipedia, Twitter, and Amazon. Each of these sites has millions of hits daily and the color scheme and layout can be recognized instantly. This project focuses on the styling for these nine of the top America websites. It duplicates the look of each site and applies it to a common page.

Each of these sites has development teams of hundreds or thousands of people working behind the scenes, to create a unique user experience for its visitors. The content being displayed and the user's needs drive the design for each site and are continually being updates.

The idea behind the project is to apply the most recognizable styling techniques each of these sites use to a standard webpage and see how well each site's styling transfers. In this interactive project, a user, one can apply an entire site's styling to the webpage, or pick and choose elements from each site. This is supposed to be a thought experiment to explore how well website design can be abstracted, adapted, and applied outside of its common usage.
