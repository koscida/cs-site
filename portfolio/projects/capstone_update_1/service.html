Creative Brief and Concept Studies

The USCSA Ski Team at CU Boulder enjoys strong, dedicated leadership under its longtime director, Paul Rozsypal. However, the team lacks a cohesive, broad branding strategy that links together the many elements that compose the program. The team is not supported financially by CU; thus, its source of income is fees charged of the members of the team. Recruiting new members and retaining old members is essential to the survival of the USCSA team. As a brand selling itself to the student body, the team needs a unified, ubiquitous style of branding that identifies it to prospective racers. In this project, we will attempt to link many aspects of the team to one another by creating similar, recognizable elements across different forms of media.

These forms of media include the team's website, flyers around campus, business cards, and stickers. The website is maintained by a CU graduate in Japan and is somewhat of a legacy system from 2005. The site contains a lot of great information on the team and resources, but feels dated. We will build a supplementary, more stylish site geared towards students using mostly HTML and PHP. This site will be hosted off the Redwood server on an independent domain. The flyers we create in InDesign will be posted around campus advertising the recruiting meetings occurring throughout the fall. Our team has obtained the necessary requirements for flyers posted on CU boards regarding neon dyes, paper weight, etc. as well as the copyright rules surrounding the use of the CU logo and buffalo. The business cards will be fancy-looking, simplistic cards that Paul can hand to prospective recruits. They will likely show the logo we design and the web address in the same color scheme that we establish on the site and flyers. Finally, the stickers will be designed in the same fashion   flashy little advertisements for the ski team. The cards and stickers will be designed in Photoshop and InDesign.

Requirements

For your first major project in Capstone, you will form teams of 2-4 and create a client-based, digital media project. Your team will seek out a client somewhere on campus and develop a project specific for their needs.

The client your group chooses to work with will be up to you but must be directly affiliated with the University of Colorado. Campus departments and programs as well as student groups and organizations are the recommended starting point in terms of seeking out a client. The digital media project you create for your client will be determined by your specific clients needs. Projects must be digital in nature but can range anywhere from poster/print campaigns to web site design to video/animation production and beyond.

When determining your client and project, be mindful of the scope of this project and the amount of time your team will have to work on it. Your team will have the first 9 weeks of the semester to fully develop, create, complete and implement this project. DO NOT choose a project that can be completed by one person in one week OR a project that will require more than 8-9 weeks to fully complete. It will be the responsibility of your team to schedule weekly meetings with your client to gather necessary materials and update them on the project during development.
